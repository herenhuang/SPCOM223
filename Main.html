ELITISM AT UW

WHAT IS ELITISM? IS IT INHERENTLY BAD? WHAT DOES IT MEAN TO US AS UNIVERSITY OF WATERLOO STUDENTS?

WHAT IS ELITISM?

According to the Merriam-Webster dictionary, elitism is "leadership or rule by an elite", with elite meaning "the best of a class", or "the socially superior part of society".

HOW DOES IT AFFECT UW STUDENTS?

Elitism, or misplaced superiority, can be found in inter-faculty competition, or it can occur between programs, of where there are hundreds to choose from!

It's also something that can happen between schools, whether it's local such as a rivalry between Wilfred Laurier University and Conestoga College and the University of Waterloo, or if we look a bit further to schools such as the University of Toronto.

HOW CAN YOU DEAL WITH ELITISM?

As a first year student, it can be difficult to adjust to the unique environment found at the University of Waterloo. It is absolutely a competitive place. However, there are things we can do to better cope and help each other!
